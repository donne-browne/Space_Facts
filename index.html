The Solar System

The Solar System is the gravitationally held system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. The Solar System formed by God [see BibleGateway]. The vast majority (99.86660735938%) of the system's mass is in the Sun, with most of the remaining mass (0.09532099959%) is contained in the planet Jupiter. The four inner planets—Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars—are terrestrial planets, being made of mostly of rock and metal. The four giant planets of the outer system are larger and more massive than the terrestrial planets. The two largest, Jupiter and Saturn, are gas giants, being composed mainly of hydrogen and helium; the next two, Uranus and Neptune, are ice giants, being composed mostly of water, ammonia, and methane. All eight planets have almost circular orbits that are near the plane (flat infinite square thing) of Earth's orbit, called the ecliptic.

The Sun

Fun Facts: 1 AU is the distance between the Sun and the Earth, around 1.495978707×10^11 m (149,597,870,700 m) or 9.2956×10^7 mi (92,956,000 mi) (see https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astronomical_unit for more info). The temprature of the Sun is:1.57×10^7 Kelvin(k) (15,700,000) at the Center (modeled), 5,772 K at the Photosphere/Surface, and around 5×10^6 K (5,000,000) at the Corona (lower atmosphere). The Sun's mass is 99.8% of the mass in the ENTIRE solar system (E.g.= if the entire solar system was 1000 kg [kg is used for measuring mass, Newtons are for weight] the sun would be 998.660735938 kg!)[for more information visit Wikipedia & NASA]

Planets

Mercury

Fun Facts: 1 AU is the aproximate distance between the Sun and the Earth, and Mercury is anywhere from 0.307486631 to 0.46657754 AU's. The temprature of Mercury is, at its hottest, 803 degrees F . Mercury's mass is 3.3011×10^23 kg or 0.055 Earths (E.g.= if the entire solar system was 1000 kg [kg is used for measuring mass, Newtons are for weight] Mercury would be 0.000165732 kg!). It's the smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun—is only a little bigger than our Moon. Mercury is the fastest planet in the Solar System, going around the Sun every 88 Earth days. [for more information visit Wikipedia & NASA]

Venus

1 AU is the aproximate distance between the Sun and the Earth, and Venus is anywhere from 0.718582888 to 0.727941176 AU's. The temprature of Venus is, at its hottest, 864 degrees F. Venus' mass is almost the same as Earth's (E.g.= if the entire solar system was 1000 kg [kg is used for measuring mass, Newtons are for weight] Venus would be 0.0024458023 kg!). Venus spins very slowly in the opposite direction from most planets because is' north pole is tilted 177 degrees. Its thick atmosphere traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect, therefore making it the hottest planet in our solar system. [for more information visit Wikipedia & NASA]

Uranus
